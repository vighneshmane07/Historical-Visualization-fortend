Raigad Fort, located in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India, is a significant historical fort that served as the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Here is the historical information you requested:

### 1. History
Raigad Fort has a rich history intertwined with the rise of the Maratha Empire. Originally known as "Rahaagad" meaning "the invincible castle," the fort was significant due to its strategic location. It commanded the trade routes and was an important military bastion. Shivaji Maharaj captured the fort in 1656, and after several enhancements, he made it his capital in 1674 when he was crowned as the Chhatrapati (Emperor) of the Maratha Empire. Over the years, the fort became a symbol of Maratha power and pride.

### 2. Who Built It
While the fort existed before Shivaji's rule, it was extensively renovated and expanded under his direction. Shivaji Maharaj realized its potential after capturing it and made significant alterations to upgrade its defenses and facilities. The fortification and construction work continued under his administration, with many features being added by his engineers and architects.

### 3. Year of Construction
While Raigad Fort existed in some form during earlier kingdoms, Shivaji began its refurbishment and expansion around 1656. The major work to transform it into a formidable fort continued throughout his rule, particularly in the late 1660s and early 1670s, leading up to Shivaji's coronation in 1674.

### 4. Important Facts
- **Elevation and Location**: Raigad Fort is situated on a hill at an altitude of about 1,300 meters (4,300 ft) in the Western Ghats, making it a natural fortification.
  
- **Architecture**: The fort features traditional Maratha architecture and is known for its impressive gateways, fortified walls, and royal palaces. Significant structures within the fort include the Rajwada (the King's residence), the main marketplace, and several temples.

- **Historical Events**: Raigad was not only the capital but also a site of significant events in Maratha history, including the coronation of Shivaji Maharaj and his death in 1680. After Shivaji's death, the fort was the focal point of several battles and political activities.

- **Access**: The fort can be reached by a steep path and has over 1,400 steps (the "Raigad Gavath" path). Alternatively, there is a cable car service for tourists offering a panoramic view of the region.

- **Significance**: Raigad Fort has become a symbol of Maratha valor and is important in the study of military architecture and history in India. It is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site under the "Hill forts of Rajasthan."

- **Modern Conservation**: The fort is now a protected monument and is a popular tourist destination, attracting history enthusiasts, trekkers, and tourists wanting to learn about the Maratha Empire.

Raigad stands today as a towering testament to the resilience and military prowess of Shivaji Maharaj and the Maratha Empire, symbolizing the rich cultural heritage of Maharashtra.
