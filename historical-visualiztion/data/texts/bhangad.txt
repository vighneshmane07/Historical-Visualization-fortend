Bhangarh Fort, often referred to simply as Bhangarh, is a historic site situated in the Alwar district of Rajasthan, India. The fort has garnered significant attention due to its intricate architecture, historical importance, and the myths and legends surrounding it.

### 1. History:
Bhangarh Fort was built in the 16th century, specifically in the year 1573. The fort is located near the Sariska Tiger Reserve and is nestled in the Aravalli Range. Historically, the site was known as a thriving town and is said to have had a prosperous existence. Bhangarh was built with a strategic plan for military defense and to serve as a residence for the Rajputs.

The fort is famous for its well-planned layout, which includes temples, palaces, and residential areas. However, the fort is also rumored to be haunted, which has contributed to its notoriety over the years.

### 2. Who Built It:
Bhangarh Fort was commissioned by Raja Madho Singh I, a younger brother of the legendary ruler Man Singh, who was one of the trusted generals in the court of the Mughal emperor Akbar. Raja Madho Singh I served as a chief of Amber and played a significant role in the establishment of the fort and the town of Bhangarh, after which the site was named.

### 3. Year of Construction:
The fort was constructed in 1573, during a time of relative stability and prosperity in the region. The architectural style reflects the Rajput style of fort design, characterized by intricate workmanship and a blend of functionality and aesthetics.

### 4. Important Facts:
- **Architectural Marvel**: Bhangarh Fort is known for its beautiful temples, palaces, and several gates. The fort is designed as a citadel and contains more than 50 temples, along with royal residences and marketplaces.
  
- **Haunted Reputation**: Bhangarh is often referred to as one of the most haunted places in India. The fort is surrounded by legends, particularly tales surrounding a sorcerer named Singhia who is said to have fallen in love with Ratnavati, the princess of Bhangarh. According to the legend, he cast a spell on her, but she countered it, leading to his demise. It is said that his spirit still roams the fort, leading to its haunted reputation.

- **Archaeological Importance**: The fort and the surrounding structures are protected under the Archaeological Survey of India. It draws numerous visitors, including tourists, historians, and adventurers.

- **Government Restrictions**: Due to its haunted reputation, the Archaeological Survey of India has prohibited entry into the fort after sunset and before sunrise, adding to the aura of mystery surrounding it.

- **Cultural Significance**: The fort is not only important for its architectural beauty but also serves as a significant reminder of the history and culture of the Rajputs in Rajasthan.

- **Tourist Attraction**: Bhangarh Fort attracts many visitors each year, including paranormal enthusiasts, tourists, and those interested in Indian history and architecture.

Bhangarh Fort remains a captivating site that intertwines history, architecture, and folklore, making it a unique destination in Rajasthan.
