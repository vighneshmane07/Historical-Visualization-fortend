Kedarnath refers primarily to Kedarnath Temple, a significant Hindu pilgrimage site situated in the Indian state of Uttarakhand in the Himalayas. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, which are highly revered shrines in the Hindu faith.

### 1. History
Kedarnath has a rich historical and mythological background. According to ancient texts, the site was established after the Mahabharata epic, where the Pandavas sought Lord Shiva's blessings for redemption after the Kurukshetra war. It is believed that Lord Shiva took the form of a bull and disappeared into the ground, which is now the site of the Kedarnath temple.

The temple has been mentioned in various ancient scriptures, including the Skanda Purana, which describes the significance of the location. Due to its extreme altitude and weather conditions, Kedarnath has endured many natural calamities, including floods and earthquakes over the centuries, yet the temple has remained a center of faith and worship.

### 2. Who built it
The construction of the Kedarnath Temple is attributed to Adi Shankaracharya, the inspirational philosopher and theologian who revitalized Hinduism in the early 8th century CE. He is said to have played a crucial role in establishing the temple and promoting the worship of Lord Shiva at this site. However, it is also believed that the temple was built on a much older site, as references to the significance of Kedarnath can be found in ancient texts.

### 3. Year of construction
While the exact year of the temple's establishment is not definitively documented, it is generally accepted that Adi Shankaracharya's contributions occurred around the 8th century CE, which places the construction during that period. The temple's structure has gone through various phases and renovations over the centuries, influenced by monks and devotees.

### 4. Important facts
- **Location**: Kedarnath Temple is situated at an elevation of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) above sea level, making it one of the highest Shiva temples in India.
- **Accessibility**: The temple is accessible only during the months of April to November, as it is covered in heavy snow during winter months, making it inaccessible.
- **Architecture**: The temple features a unique architecture style, characterized by the use of large stones and wooden elements, typical of traditional Himalayan styles.
- **Pilgrimage**: Kedarnath is part of the Char Dham Yatra, which includes Badrinath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri. It attracts thousands of pilgrims every year.
- **Natural calamities**: The temple and surrounding area were severely affected by the devastating floods of June 2013, which led to significant loss of life and property. Extensive reconstruction efforts were undertaken after the disaster.
- **Reverence**: Kedarnath is venerated by millions of Hindus, and the deity, known here as Kedar, is considered highly auspicious and powerful. Pilgrims believe that visiting Kedarnath grants liberation (moksha) and salvation.

These elements contribute to the historical and religious significance of Kedarnath, making it a cornerstone of Hinduism in India.
