### Purandar Fort: Historical Overview

#### 1. History
Purandar Fort, also known as Purandhar Durg, is a significant fort located in the Pune district of Maharashtra, India. The fort has a rich history that is intertwined with the rise of the Maratha Empire. It played a crucial role during the 17th century, particularly during the rule of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who is credited with establishing the Maratha Empire.

The fort originally served as a strategic military outpost in the Deccan region and was crucial for controlling the trade routes and military movements in the area. Its location on the Sahyadri mountain range made it an ideal position for surveillance and defense. 

In 1665, after the Treaty of Purandar, which was signed between Shivaji and the Mughal Empire, the fort came under Mughal control. It was a pivotal stronghold for the Mughals for several years until it was recaptured by Shivaji Maharaj in 1670. The fort witnessed several battles and changing allegiances throughout the Maratha-Mughal wars before eventually falling into disrepair.

In later years, the fort was under British control post the Anglo-Maratha Wars, and it served as a military outpost until it was eventually abandoned. Today, Purandar Fort stands as a historical structure and a site for tourism.

#### 2. Who Built It

Purandar Fort was believed to have been constructed during the rule of the Adil Shahi dynasty in the 15th century. The exact date of its initial construction is not clearly documented, but it was later developed and fortified by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and his forces during his campaign to consolidate Maratha power in western India. Shivaji is credited with adding multiple structures and enhancing the defenses of the fort.

#### 3. Year of Construction

The initial construction of Purandar Fort can be traced back to the 15th century, during the reign of the Bahmani Sultanate and later the Adil Shahi dynasty. However, Shivaji Maharaj significantly enhanced and expanded it during the 17th century, especially after he established his control over the region.

#### 4. Important Facts

- **Strategic Importance**: The fort's elevation at about 4,500 feet (1,400 meters) above sea level gives it a commanding view of the surrounding area, making it strategically vital for controlling the traffic and movements in the western Deccan.

- **Architectural Features**: Purandar Fort comprises two main forts – Purandar and the adjoining "Ajinkyatara Fort." It includes several structures, such as bastions, water cisterns, and temples. There are also significant remnants of old cannons, gates, and military architecture.

- **Cultural Significance**: The fort holds cultural importance as it was the site of many Maratha victories and strategies employed during the conflicts with the Mughals.

- **Current Status**: Today, Purandar Fort is a popular destination for trekkers and history enthusiasts. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and is known for its trekking trails.

- **Commemoration of Battles**: Purandar Fort is remembered for the battle strategies devised by Shivaji Maharaj, and an important historical event related to the fort is the signing of the Treaty of Purandar in 1665.

In summary, Purandar Fort is a landmark of historical significance, reflecting the glory of the Maratha Empire and the military ingenuity of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Its storied past and architectural splendor make it a distinguished part of India's rich heritage.
