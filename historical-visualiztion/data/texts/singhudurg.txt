### Singhadurg Fort: Historical Overview

#### 1. History
Singhadurg Fort, also known as Sinhagad Fort, is located near Pune in the western Indian state of Maharashtra. It holds significant historical importance, particularly in the context of Maratha history and the Deccan region. The fort was a prominent military fortification and has witnessed various battles and power shifts over the centuries.

The fort is famously associated with Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire. It featured prominently in the Maratha battles against the Mughal Empire, serving as a strategic military outpost. The fort has a rich history that includes sieges, conquests, and tales of valor.

#### 2. Who Built It
The original structure of Singhagad is believed to have been built by the first Rashtrakuta kings around the 12th century. However, it gained prominence when Shivaji Maharaj captured it in 1670. Shivaji transformed Singhagad into a formidable fort, enhancing its defenses and using it as a base for various military campaigns.

#### 3. Year of Construction
The fort's initial construction dates back to the 12th century, but Shivaji captured and fortified it in 1670, marking its significance in the history of Maratha warfare.

#### 4. Important Facts
- **Strategic Location**: Singhagad Fort is perched atop a hill approximately 1,312 meters (4,301 feet) above sea level and overlooks the Pune valley. Its strategic location made it a prime site for surveillance and defense.
  
- **Famous Battles**: The fort is known for the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, where Tanaji Malusare, a trusted lieutenant of Shivaji Maharaj, played a crucial role. The battle was fought to recapture the fort from the Mughal forces led by Udaybhan. Tanaji famously exclaimed, "Gad aala, pan Singh gela" (“The fort is captured, but the lion is dead”), after losing his life in the battle, which became a symbol of bravery and sacrifice.

- **Architectural Features**: Singhagad boasts impressive architecture, including stone walls, bastions, and gates that demonstrate military engineering of the time. Notable structures within the fort include the tomb of Tanaji Malusare, a Bhairav temple, and various water tanks.

- **Tourist Attraction**: Today, Singhagad Fort is a popular trekking destination and attracts history enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and tourists interested in the rich Maratha legacy. The scenic views from the fort add to its charm.

- **Cultural Importance**: The fort is a significant cultural symbol in Maharashtra and has featured in various Marathi literature, folklore, and songs. Its history of resistance against Mughal authority is celebrated in Maratha folklore and serves as an inspiration for valor and courage.

Singhadurg Fort continues to be a monument of historical significance and cultural pride for the people of Maharashtra and stands as a testament to the legacy of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and the Maratha Empire.
