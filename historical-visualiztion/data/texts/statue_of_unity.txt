The Statue of Unity is a colossal statue located in the Indian state of Gujarat. It is dedicated to Vallabhbhai Patel (1875-1950), an important leader in the Indian independence movement and the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of India.

### 1. History
The idea for the Statue of Unity emerged as a means of honoring Vallabhbhai Patel for his pivotal role in unifying the states of India after independence. Known as the "Iron Man of India," Patel played a crucial role in integrating over 500 princely states into the Indian Union. The concept was announced during the centenary celebrations of Patel's birth in 2013 by then Prime Minister Narendra Modi, who emphasized Patel's vision of a united India. The project also aimed to promote tourism and economic development in the region.

### 2. Who Built It
The statue was designed by Indian sculptor Ram V. Sutar, who is renowned for his work in large-scale sculptures. The construction was carried out by Larsen & Toubro (L&T), one of India's largest infrastructure development companies. The project was supported by various state and central governmental bodies, with Prime Minister Modi playing an instrumental role in its conception and promotion.

### 3. Year of Construction
The construction began in 2014, and the statue was inaugurated on October 31, 2018, coinciding with Patel's birth anniversary. The project took about 46 months from the groundbreaking to the inauguration, involving extensive planning, engineering challenges, and the collaborative efforts of thousands of workers.

### 4. Important Facts
- **Height**: The Statue of Unity stands at a staggering height of 182 meters (597 feet), making it the tallest statue in the world, surpassing the Spring Temple Buddha in China.
- **Location**: It is situated on a river island near Kevadia in the Narmada district of Gujarat, about 3.2 km from the Sardar Sarovar Dam, and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
- **Design**: The statue is made of bronze-clad reinforced concrete and features a detailed sculpture of Patel in traditional Indian attire, signifying his humility and connection to the people.
- **Visitor Facilities**: The site features an exhibition hall, a museum showcasing Patel's life and contributions, and a viewing gallery at a height of 153 meters, which provides visitors with breathtaking views of the Narmada river and the surrounding hills.
- **Environmental Concerns**: The construction of the statue involved considerable environmental scrutiny, especially concerning its impact on the Narmada river ecosystem and surrounding wildlife.
- **Tourism Impact**: Since its inauguration, the Statue of Unity has significantly contributed to local tourism, attracting millions of visitors each year and boosting the economy of the region.

The Statue of Unity serves not only as a monument commemorating Vallabhbhai Patel's legacy but also as a symbol of India’s commitment to unity and progress.
